## Judgment (after retry)

**Strengths:**
- **Palette is exact match to PRD** — every PRD color is present and dominant.
- Daisy flowers (salon namesake) = strong brand reinforcement. Future marketers will love this — every review can mention "the salon with the daisy logo".
- Asymmetric bento composition = directly matches the design-quality rule "Grid-breaking editorial or bento composition where appropriate".
- No text artifacts on retry — clean canvas.
- Strong poster-art confidence — instantly readable as a "brand", not a "person".
- Slight grain texture = tactile, not slick; reinforces warmth despite the bold palette.
- Works for both LIGHT and DARK mode without re-coloring (charcoal already dark).

**Weaknesses:**
- Loses the "warm hospitality" personality — feels more like a fashion brand than a neighborhood salon.
- Doesn't communicate "personal touch" — abstract illustration is the opposite of candid.
- Less likely to differentiate from other beauty salon websites (many use similar poster-art style).
- "Modern bloom" energy may skew slightly too young (vs. PRD's 18-45 range including older women).
- Stylized = less authentic. The reviews consistently praise "the real Mba Debby" / personal branding — this concept obscures that.

**Fit with PRD signals:**
- "warm" brand voice: PARTIAL — modern, not warm
- "golden hour lighting": N/A — illustration, not photo
- "natural, candid photo style": N/A — flat illustration by design
- "women-owned, personal touch": WEAK — no human element
- "host hospitality signals": NONE

**Verdict:** Strong as a brand identity statement, weaker as a "neighborhood salon" statement. Beautiful illustration, but trades personality for graphic punch.
